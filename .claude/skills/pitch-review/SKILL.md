# /pitch-review — proposal review by interview

Review a proposal, business plan, or pitch deck against the seven lenses in `concepts/06-proposal-anatomy.md`. **Don't red-pen it for them — ask the questions that make them see the weak spot.** Give the synthesized verdict only at the end.

## Start

- Ask for the material: a file, a paste, or a spoken summary. If it's long, ask what the single ask of the proposal is first.
- Say which seven lenses you'll run, one line each, so they know the yardstick.

## The seven lenses (as questions)

Walk them one or two at a time — not as a checklist dump. For each, ask before you judge.

1. **Conclusion first?** "Where does a reader first learn your core point — page 1, or page 12? Is the proof up front or in an appendix?"
2. **Battlefield choice.** "Are you fighting the incumbent where it's strong? What axis did you redraw the problem onto — and can you actually win there?"
3. **Logic-tree → strategy axis.** "What's the split that organizes your strategies? Does the set close the funnel, or only open it (acquisition with no retention)?"
4. **Target × strategy alignment.** "Are the targets you name early the same ones your later strategies serve? Where do they drift apart?"
5. **Slide grammar.** "Does each key page carry a one-line conclusion up top and a one-line implication at the bottom, or does the reader have to infer the point?"
6. **A concept that bookends.** "Is there a single line that opens and closes the deck? What is it?"
7. **Feasibility signals.** "What in here says 'this will actually ship' — a real benchmark, real usage, a lifecycle-based roadmap? Or is it all intention?"

## The CSAC cross-check

Alongside the seven lenses, probe the underlying logic:

- **Clarification:** "Is the problem it solves the *real* problem, or a symptom?"
- **Structuring:** "Is the core claim conclusion-shaped and falsifiable, or a vague aspiration?"
- **Analysis:** "For the biggest claim — what evidence backs it, and what shape is that evidence?"
- Watch for the quantitative caution: if the ask *needs* numbers and the deck is all qualitative structure, name that gap.

## Verdict

At the end, and only then, synthesize:

- **Strongest 1–2 lenses** (what's working — be specific about why).
- **Weakest 1–2 lenses** (the load-bearing weaknesses, ranked).
- **The one change** that would move the proposal most, and why.

Offer to save the review to `cases/YYYY-MM-DD-topic-review.md` if it went deep.
